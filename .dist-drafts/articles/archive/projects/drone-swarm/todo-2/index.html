Project LOCUST#

Localized Operation and Coordination Unit for Swarm Tactics — LOCUST

Overview#

A military-grade drone swarm system built around two drone types, purpose-built for reconnaissance, surveillance, tunnel/underground operations, and target tracking. Optimized for anti-terrorism and hostile-environment field use.

  • Async drones (×4, belt-worn + backpack auto-launch) — full sensor suite, independently capable, used for recon, tunnel ops, dark environments, through-wall sensing, object deployment, and directed surveillance.
  • Sync drones (×12, suitcase) — vision-first, centrally coordinated, used for perimeter coverage, formation surveillance sweeps, multi-angle subject lock, and distributed CSI sensing mesh.

Both fleets connect to a computer backpack worn by the operator. The backpack features a motorized auto-launch rack that flips up on command and deploys async drones automatically — no manual handling needed. A head-mounted display (HMD) shows drone feeds and controls. Motion gloves (or Quest 3 hand tracking) allow precision manual control. Solar panels on the suitcase and backpack extend field endurance without shore power.

All cameras across all drones are military-grade dual-mode: switchable day/night with IR-cut filter removal, and thermal imaging is standard across all drones (not optional) for heat signature detection in any lighting condition.

The onboard AI is called LOCUST — all voice commands are prefixed with its name. Example: “LOCUST, send the swarm to circle that building and scan for signs of life.”

Acronym: Localized Operation and Coordination Unit for Swarm Tactics

Drone Types#

Shared Capabilities (Both Async and Sync)#

Features present on every drone in the fleet:

Feature Notes
GPS positioning Async: triangulation + last-known location. Sync: formation positioning.
LoRa communication Backpack is master node; all drones share the same network with unique addresses.
USB-C calibration Belt/suitcase plugs into backpack RPi. All drones flash ID, LoRa address, LED colour, flight profile in one shot. No Bluetooth needed.
WiFi CSI sensing (ESP32-S3) Through-wall presence, motion, breathing, activity detection. See WiFi Sensing section.

Async Drones (Belt + Backpack Rack, ×4)#

Full-featured drones for individual, autonomous tasks. Carried on the belt and auto-launched from the backpack rack.

Async-exclusive capabilities:

  • Military-grade dual-mode camera with switchable IR-cut filter (Sony IMX415 or equivalent)
  • Thermal camera (FLIR Lepton 3.5 µmodule) — standard, not optional
  • IR LED illumination (850 nm, ×4) — night vision, invisible to the naked eye
  • UV LED (395 nm) — forensic scanning
  • White LED flashlight — caves, tunnels, dark interiors
  • Time-of-flight sensor (VL53L5CX) — obstacle avoidance + gesture/pointing triangulation
  • SDR module (optional) — passive RF listening, receive-only
  • Acconeer XM125 60 GHz pulsed radar — high-resolution through-wall sensing; complements WiFi CSI
  • Object release mechanism — servo-actuated payload holder; command-triggered drop via LOCUST or glove gesture. Used for deploying trackers, markers, or other payloads.
  • License plate recognition (LPR) via Hailo-8
  • Person re-identification across drone feeds
  • Fall / incident detection

Tunnel/Underground Recon Mode:

Standard GPS fails underground. Async drones switch to a dead-reckoning + optical flow navigation stack for tunnel operations:

  • Optical flow sensor (PMW3901) tracks ground movement for position estimation
  • Time-of-flight (VL53L5CX) for tunnel wall/ceiling clearance
  • LoRa signal remains active as long as line-of-sight to backpack or a relay drone is maintained
  • IR LED illumination + thermal provides full sensor coverage in complete darkness
  • Relay chaining: one async drone holds position at tunnel entrance as a LoRa repeater; inner drones communicate through it

Voice command: “LOCUST, tunnel mode” — switches from GPS nav to optical flow, enables full IR/thermal, reduces throttle for confined-space maneuvering.

Palm Auto-Return: On low battery or voice command “LOCUST, return”:

  1. Drone navigates toward backpack GPS coordinates
  2. Switches to downward-facing camera
  3. Hailo-8 detects open palm in the frame
  4. Hovers ~10 cm above palm — grab it or let it land

Async drones never die mid-air from neglect.

Sync Drones (Suitcase, ×12)#

Lightweight, vision-first drones for coordinated swarm operations. All heavy AI runs on the backpack — not on each drone.

Design philosophy: Centralize AI on the backpack Hailo-8. Each sync drone carries a military-grade dual-mode camera and compact thermal sensor. Hailo-8 handles depth estimation, obstacle avoidance, and formation positioning centrally.

Sync-exclusive capabilities:

  • Military-grade dual-mode camera (Sony IMX335 or equivalent) with switchable IR-cut filter — full day/night operation
  • Compact thermal sensor (MLX90640) — 32×24 thermal array; heat signature detection across full perimeter mesh
  • 8× addressable RGB LEDs (WS2812B) — formation markers, identification
  • IR LED (850 nm, simplified) — basic IR illumination for night formation shots and stealth ops
  • Suitcase auto-dock — IR beacon homing + magnetic pogo-pin capture, no manual plug-in

Sync use cases:

  • Flight formations — circling a perimeter, geometric patterns, synchronized movement
  • Multi-angle surveillance — surround a target from multiple simultaneous viewpoints
  • Perimeter sensing mesh — station drones around a building; CSI data from all nodes + thermal feeds fused into occupancy map
  • Building approach recon — swarm fans out ahead to clear approach routes
  • Relay networks — extend communication range across large areas
  • Light shows and LED choreography (non-operational use cases)

Suitcase Auto-Dock: On low battery or command:

  1. Drone receives suitcase GPS (broadcast by suitcase MCU over LoRa)
  2. Navigates to suitcase — works even if it has moved
  3. Switches to IR beacon homing for final approach
  4. Magnets capture and seat drone in bay precisely
  5. Pogo pins begin charging — no manual plug-in

Backpack Auto-Launch Rack#

The backpack carries a motorized 4-slot async drone rack on the operator’s upper back. At rest, the rack lies flat against the backpack frame. On command, a motorized arm flips the rack up to a near-vertical launch angle, presenting all 4 async drones for auto-takeoff.

Launch sequence:

  1. “LOCUST, deploy” — rack motor flips up to 70° angle
  2. All 4 async drones power up, run self-check
  3. Drones launch simultaneously or in sequence (configurable)
  4. Rack folds back flat once all drones are airborne
  5. Drones navigate to assigned positions autonomously

Return sequence:

  1. “LOCUST, return” — async drones navigate to backpack GPS
  2. Rack motor flips up to receive position
  3. Drones hover above rack; operator confirms each return
  4. Drone lands on rack cradle; pogo pins connect for charging

Rack hardware:

  • 12V DC gear motor or heavy-duty servo (MG996R or equivalent) for arm actuation
  • Dual-axis pivot mount on MOLLE backpack frame attachment
  • Spring-loaded safety latch — prevents accidental deployment
  • 4 TPU cradle mounts with pogo-pin connectors, same interface as belt bays
  • Triggered via backpack MCU on LOCUST voice command or dedicated physical button

Belt carry still available: If the rack is undesirable for a given operation, all 4 async drones clip to the belt as normal. Belt and rack are interchangeable; drones use the same USB-C/pogo interface on both.

Object Release System#

Each async drone carries a servo-actuated payload holder on its underside. The mechanism grips a small payload during flight and drops it on command.

Trigger methods:

  • Voice: “LOCUST, drop payload, [drone ID]”
  • Glove: Index finger extended + double thumb-tap (configurable)
  • HMD tap on drone icon

Design:

  • Micro servo (SG90 or MG90S) actuates a retention arm that releases the payload
  • Payload cavity: ~40 mm × 40 mm × 20 mm (accommodates GPS trackers, signal emitters, chemical markers, or similar)
  • Servo draws from drone power rail; weight penalty ~6–8 g including mount
  • No external trigger wire — fully integrated with drone FC via PWM output

Payload ideas (non-weaponized):

  • GPS/RF trackers for tagging a vehicle or container
  • Chemical/dye markers for location marking
  • Audible or signal emitters for distraction/positioning

Through-Wall Sensing#

Two complementary technologies work together on the async drone and as a distributed mesh on the sync fleet.

WiFi CSI (ESP32-S3) — Long Range, Any Building#

WiFi Channel State Information sensing reads how 2.4 GHz signals are disturbed as they pass through walls and interact with moving objects.

What it detects:

Capability Status Notes
Human presence ✅ Reliable Works through drywall at 5–15 m
Motion detection ✅ Reliable Sensitive enough to detect breathing
Breathing rate ✅ Reliable Non-contact vital sign monitoring
Activity class (walking/sitting/falling) ✅ Reliable CNN model on CSI spectrograms
Rough zone location ✅ Yes Multiple nodes improve accuracy
Body pose / skeleton ⚠️ Experimental Requires per-environment ML training
Number of people ⚠️ Partial Reliable to ~3–5 per node
Images through walls ❌ Never CSI is signal distortion, not a camera
Through metal / foil ❌ Blocked Near-zero signal penetration

Wall penetration range:

Wall Type Range
Drywall / plasterboard 5–15 m
Brick 3–10 m
Reinforced concrete 3–8 m
Glass 10–18 m
Metal / foil insulation ~0

Sensing modes:

  1. Active — drone transmits and reads its own reflected signal. Single-drone, best for 3–8 m walls.
  2. Passive — drone reads distortion in ambient WiFi. No transmission needed.
  3. Multi-drone mesh — the most powerful mode. Deploy 4–8 sync drones around a building: one transmits, others receive from different angles. All CSI streams go back to the backpack via LoRa; Hailo-8 fuses them into a room-level occupancy map displayed in the HMD.

Hard limits:

  • Cannot produce images through walls — ever. This is signal distortion analysis, not imaging.
  • Static people are harder — breathing still detects, but activity recognition drops.
  • Environment-specific — furniture layout affects multipath. Presence and motion detection work out of the box; body pose requires 10–15 min environment training per location.

Software stack:

  • Drone: ESP32-S3 running esp-csi (Espressif open source). Compressed CSI packets sent to backpack via LoRa burst.
  • Backpack: Python/Rust CSI processor on RPi 5. ML inference on Hailo-8.

Acconeer XM125 60 GHz Radar — Async Drone Only#

Miniature pulsed coherent radar (PCR) that emits short 60 GHz bursts and reads reflections directly — like a miniature radar unit.

Capability XM125 Radar WiFi CSI
Requires building to have WiFi ❌ No Passive mode: yes
Works through metal-rich walls ⚠️ Better ❌ Blocked by metal
Point-and-read (single drone) ✅ Yes — up to 7 m ✅ Yes
Breathing detection ✅ Yes — high accuracy ✅ Yes
Distributed mesh sensing ❌ Single-drone only ✅ Multi-drone
Range 7 m (presence), 20 m (lens) 5–15 m (drywall)

Use together: WiFi CSI handles wide-area perimeter mesh (sync fleet). XM125 on async drone provides high-confidence, directed point-sensing when hovering close to a specific target. Fused on Hailo-8 for higher-confidence reads.

LOCUST AI — Voice Activation#

All commands are prefixed with “LOCUST” to avoid triggering on normal conversation.

Example syntax:

“LOCUST, send the swarm to circle that building and scan for signs of life.” “LOCUST, tunnel mode, drone alpha.” “LOCUST, drop payload, drone bravo.”

Core voice commands:

Command Action
“LOCUST, deploy” Rack flips up; all 4 async drones launch automatically.
“LOCUST, return” Async: palm auto-return to rack or belt. Sync: suitcase auto-dock.
“LOCUST, deploy solar” Suitcase solar panels unfold + begin sun tracking.
“LOCUST, night mode [on/off]” Override auto-IR. Activates IR camera + illumination across fleet.
“LOCUST, thermal mode [on/off]” Enable thermal overlay on HMD across active feeds.
“LOCUST, scan that building” Async drone navigates to pointed target → CSI + radar sweep → HMD report.
“LOCUST, tunnel mode [drone ID]” Switches specified drone to optical flow nav + full IR/thermal + confined-space throttle profile.
“LOCUST, track that person” Hailo-8 face/body lock on HMD reticle target.
“LOCUST, cover the perimeter” Sync fleet takes distributed perimeter stations; breach detection goes active.
“LOCUST, sweep the area” Swarm runs grid search pattern; Hailo-8 flags detected persons across all thermal + camera feeds.
“LOCUST, drop payload, [drone ID]” Actuates object release servo on specified drone.
“LOCUST, pull up feed [drone ID]” Displays that drone’s camera in HMD.
“LOCUST, give me control of [ID]” Activates precision glove/hand-tracking mode for that drone.
“LOCUST, record and tag” Starts geo-tagged clip on current active feed.

Gesture commands (no LOCUST prefix needed):

Gesture Action

Solar Power System#

Suitcase Solar — Primary (50W + Sun Tracking)#

The suitcase lid carries 4 modular 12.5W flexible solar panels (50W total) on motorized folding arms.

  • 4× LDR sensors per panel drive 2× SG90 servos per panel (azimuth + elevation)
  • 8 servos total, controlled by PCA9685 on suitcase MCU
  • Tracking adds ~20–30% energy harvest vs fixed-angle panels
  • Full sun: recharges ~2–3 sync drone batteries per hour
  • Panels fold flat when drones are launching or landing

Backpack Solar — Secondary (30W)#

30W foldable panel on backpack rear. Partially offsets RPi 5 + Hailo-8 draw (~15W). Optional motorized single-axis tilt servo for sun tracking.

Solar Summary#

Device Panel Peak Output Primary Benefit
Suitcase 4× 12.5W (50W) + 2-axis sun tracking ~40W effective Recharges sync drone batteries in the field
Backpack 30W foldable + optional single-axis tracking ~25W in sun Extends all-day operator endurance
Belt / Rack None — wired to backpack battery — —
Drones None — tech not viable yet — Revisit with perovskite cells

Surveillance#

What Both Drones Share#

Both async and sync drones are full surveillance platforms. Hailo-8 runs AI inference on all active feeds simultaneously. Thermal feeds are now standard across the full fleet.

Shared across all drones:

  • Face detection and real-time tracking
  • Person detection and flagging
  • Thermal heat signature detection (FLIR Lepton on async; MLX90640 on sync)
  • Dual-mode camera feed — instant day/night toggle across full fleet
  • Geo-tagged evidence logging (GPS + timestamp per clip/frame)
  • Live HMD feed display

Async-Only Surveillance#

  • Person re-identification — match appearance features across drone feeds when subject leaves view
  • License plate recognition (LPR) — reads plates at 3–6 m; logs strings in real time
  • Night surveillance — IR illumination + IR filter off = invisible to targets
  • XM125 radar — directed through-wall presence detection
  • Fall / incident detection — motionless or downed subject flagged in HMD
  • Tunnel recon — full sensor coverage underground via optical flow nav + IR + thermal

Sync-Only Surveillance#

  • Crowd counting — multiple overhead angles across large areas
  • Perimeter breach detection — virtual boundary trigger in HMD
  • Multi-angle subject lock — 4–6 drones surrounding a subject simultaneously
  • Formation surveillance sweep — grid pattern flight with Hailo-8 flagging detections in each feed
  • WiFi CSI perimeter mesh — distributed through-wall sensing of an entire building exterior
  • Thermal perimeter mesh — 12-drone ring around a structure provides full thermal coverage

Storage#

1 TB NVMe SSD on backpack RPi. 16 simultaneous drone feeds at H.264/H.265 ≈ ~25 MB/s → ~11 hours full-swarm recording per session.

Drone Belt (4-bay, Async) / Backpack Rack (4-slot, Async)#

The belt and auto-launch rack are interchangeable. Use belt for low-profile operations; use rack for rapid deployment.

  • USB-C / pogo-pin charges and calibrates all 4 async drones
  • MAX17048 fuel gauge ICs report charge status to backpack continuously
  • WS2812B status LEDs per bay
  • TPU drone cradles with USB-C guides

Drone Suitcase (12-bay, Sync)#

Hard-shell Pelican-style case. LoRa data link to backpack at all times in field.

  • 12 magnetic pogo-pin docking bays with TPU cradles
  • IR landing beacons per bay (unique modulation frequency per slot)
  • USB-C calibration hub (all 12 drones simultaneously)
  • Battery fuel gauges + status display in lid
  • GPS + LoRa for position broadcast
  • 4× modular 12.5W solar panels with 2-axis motorized sun-tracking arms
  • MPPT controller + PCA9685 servo driver + quad-LDR sun sensors

Head-Mounted Display#

Path A — Realistic: Meta Quest 3#

  • Connect to backpack RPi via USB-C link cable (lowest latency) or wirelessly via Virtual Desktop
  • App shows: drone feeds, formation controls, LOCUST voice shortcut buttons, CSI occupancy map, thermal overlay, surveillance alerts, geo-tagged evidence log, solar power status, rack deploy/return status

Path B — Delusional: Custom Smart Glasses#

  • FPGA (Lattice ECP5 or Xilinx Artix-7) — parallel graphics + sensor processing
  • Micro OLED displays (Sony ECX339A or eMagin) — one per eye + beam-splitter optics
  • Waveguide / prism optics — the expensive part; diffractive waveguides from Lumus/DigiLens need high MOQ
  • 4× OV5640 cameras — 2 front, 2 rear (arm tips) for 360° operator awareness
  • Hand tracking cameras — required for this path
  • MEMS microphones with DSP for outdoor noise cancellation
  • Optional retina scanner for biometric login
  • Custom titanium or carbon fiber frame

Motion Gloves#

One pair for precision individual drone control and object release triggering.

Primary option: Physical gloves with flex sensors. Alternative (Quest 3 path only): Quest 3 hand tracking.

Activation: Fist → open hand gesture Pause drone: Double pointer-to-thumb tap Object release: Index extended + double thumb-tap (configurable)

Per-glove components:

  • nRF52840 MCU (BLE built-in)
  • ICM-42688-P IMU — wrist roll/pitch/yaw
  • 5× Spectra Symbol flex sensors
  • LiPo 150–200 mAh in wrist band
  • ERM haptic motor — feedback for command confirm, low battery, palm-return acknowledged, payload release confirmed

Battery Strategy#

Async Drones#

  • 15–25 min per charge (full sensor + thermal payload)
  • 3 batteries per drone, hot-swapped via belt or rack
  • 4 drones × 3 batteries = 45–75 min continuous coverage
  • Palm auto-return prevents mid-air battery death

Sync Drones#

  • 20–30 min per charge (thermal adds slight draw vs previous config)
  • 2 batteries per drone; charge in suitcase between sets
  • Suitcase solar recharges between rounds

Backpack#

  • 20,000 mAh 4S LiPo: 4–8 hrs RPi 5 runtime + drone charging overhead
  • 30W solar adds meaningful top-off during all-day deployments
  • Use 18650 pack with proper BMS

Future Ideas#

  • Nuclear micro-batteries — when commercially available, flight time becomes irrelevant.
  • Solid-state LiPo — projected ~40–45 min flight at this drone scale by ~2030.
  • On-drone AI (async) — if battery tech improves, each async drone carries its own inference chip for true independence from the backpack.
  • Suitcase auto-landing with moving suitcase — suitcase broadcasts GPS continuously; drones always have a live target even while being carried.
  • Perovskite solar cells — 30–40% efficiency vs 20–22% for current monocrystalline. Would nearly double harvest.
  • IEEE 802.11bf (WiFi SENS standard) — finalized 2024. Future WiFi chips expose CSI natively as a standard feature.
  • Tunnel relay mesh — pre-position sync drones as static LoRa relays through a tunnel network; async drones navigate further underground using the relay chain.
  • LOCUST acronym refinement — current: Localized Operation and Coordination Unit for Swarm Tactics. Open to revision.
