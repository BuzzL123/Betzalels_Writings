What Is It?#

The Hydra 83 is a custom 83-key mechanical keyboard that can switch between USB HID and PS/2 via a hardware toggle. The name comes from two things:

  • 83 — the total key count
  • Hydra — the multi-headed creature of myth, representing the board’s ability to switch between protocols. In PS/2 mode it’s a standard PS/2 keyboard; in USB mode it’s a USB keyboard that also accepts a PS/2 mouse on the PS/2 connector

The Hydra is intended to be a series. Future versions may add an integrated USB hub, additional protocol modes, or other switching features.

Project LOCUST#

Localized Operation and Coordination Unit for Swarm Tactics — LOCUST

Overview#

A military-grade drone swarm system built around two drone types, purpose-built for reconnaissance, surveillance, tunnel/underground operations, and target tracking. Optimized for anti-terrorism and hostile-environment field use.

  • Async drones (×4, belt-worn + backpack auto-launch) — full sensor suite, independently capable, used for recon, tunnel ops, dark environments, through-wall sensing, object deployment, and directed surveillance.
  • Sync drones (×12, suitcase) — vision-first, centrally coordinated, used for perimeter coverage, formation surveillance sweeps, multi-angle subject lock, and distributed CSI sensing mesh.

Both fleets connect to a computer backpack worn by the operator. The backpack features a motorized auto-launch rack that flips up on command and deploys async drones automatically — no manual handling needed. A head-mounted display (HMD) shows drone feeds and controls. Motion gloves (or Quest 3 hand tracking) allow precision manual control. Solar panels on the suitcase and backpack extend field endurance without shore power.

Everyone’s Depressed (Why Modernity Leads to Depression) V0

2026-04-08
archiveimported

Focus: Pulls the depressed cult/suicidal empathy + identity crisis material (including trans stats + Talmud clarification) and fully integrates all your new bullets. Short, punchy title as requested. This stands related but separate — it explains the personal/psychological fallout of the “infective thinking” from the series.

Key Content / Chapters (short sections, easy to read or thread):

  • Depression as a Byproduct of Modernity Rates are historically high: U.S. adult depression ~18%+ in 2024–2025 (Gallup), with youth under 30 doubling since 2017 to ~27%. Adolescents show even higher symptoms. Not normal — modernity’s rapid changes (social media, disconnection, loss of traditional anchors) amplify it.
  • Depression as Consumerism / The Chemical Imbalance Myth Consumer culture promises fulfillment through acquisition and distraction but delivers emptiness — a “philosophy of futility.” The “chemical imbalance” (e.g., low serotonin) theory is a myth: major 2022+ reviews (UCL/Moncrieff et al.) found no convincing evidence linking depression to simple neurotransmitter deficits. It’s oversold, often by providers or pharma history, but reality is more complex (environment, meaning, lifestyle).
  • Genetics: Predisposition, Not Destiny (The Partial Excuse): Twin/adoption data show ~37% heritability for MDD—no single gene, just thousands of tiny variants (PRS explains <6% of variance so far). Depressed parents raise kids’ risk 2–3x via both genes and environment (rearing stress, modeling). But rates have doubled in youth since 2010 while genes haven’t changed—proving modernity (disconnection, overload, meaning vacuum) is the real accelerator. “It’s genetics” is sometimes a cop-out; the surge demands we fix the environment, not just pop pills that don’t target a proven “imbalance.”
  • Wrong Person, Wrong Environment (School System) Many are mismatched: sensitive souls in rigid, ideologically charged modern schools that prioritize feelings over resilience, or high-stimulation environments without real community/purpose. This breeds chronic stress and disconnection.
  • Do Cities (and Overcrowding) Cause Depression? (The Urban Environment Question): Yes—in developed countries, urban density links to ~30% higher depression odds via noise, pollution, fragmentation, and overcrowding (worst for low-income). It’s modernity’s “wrong environment” on steroids: too many people, too little space or green. Rural flips sometimes due to care gaps, but cities amplify the epidemic. Solution? Don’t just flee to suburbs (great if you can)—redesign cities with parks, community hubs, and lower-density living. Or choose your environment intentionally while building tradition and purpose anywhere. Fix the setting; don’t medicate the symptom.
  • A Sign of Metamorphosis (Carl Jung) Jung viewed depression not always as pure pathology but often as a catalyst for individuation — turning inward, confronting the unconscious, and renewing the personality. It can foreshadow growth or creative burst if engaged. The hidden price of resisting: sunk cost fallacy in clinging to an outdated identity or life path that no longer fits.
  • Is It Mislabeled as Distraction? (You’re Seeing Too Much) Constant input (news, social media, notifications) overwhelms — what feels like “depression” may partly be overload or hyper-awareness of a chaotic world. Not just distraction; it’s information sickness.
  • Searching for Meaning in a Meaningless World Modernity strips traditional sources of purpose (community, tradition, transcendence), leaving an “existential vacuum.” Consumerism and pleasure fill the gap poorly, fueling the mass neurotic triad (aggression, addiction, depression).
  • Is It All an Illusion? (Feelings and Thoughts as a River) Thoughts and feelings flow like a river — you can observe and choose what to engage, but they aren’t “you.” The modern identity crisis (fluid self-ID as paramount) amplifies this: “just pick and choose your identity.” Caution — choices can backstab if detached from biology, reality, or consequences. (Ties to series: “1% transgender as normal” misrepresents surge/contagion; Talmud “six genders” are rare biological edge cases for halakha, not self-ID spectrum.)
  • Does Media (Romance Movies, Social Media) Make Lonely People Depressed? (The Comparison Trap Question): Absolutely—lonely folks watch romance or “perfect” lives for escape, but it triggers social comparison (“why don’t I have that?”), deepening the vacuum and addictive “powerless” feeling. (Music hint: melancholic tracks can loop rumination or feel addictive, but they also process sadness as a signal—like Jung’s metamorphosis—if you don’t become the emotion.) For emotional thinkers (women, liberal teens where rates hit hardest): feelings aren’t “you”—observe them like a river, then choose real-world action over screens. Solution? Curate or cut consumption; seek face-to-face connections and purpose. Use sadness as fuel for growth, not identity. Depression isn’t the end—it’s a call to fill the meaning gap.
  • It’s Infectious + It’s a Cry for Meaning Depressive thinking spreads via social networks and culture (infective empathy loops). At root, it’s often a cry for meaning in a world that offers none. Effective response: turn toward purpose, tradition, real connections — not endless affirmation or chemical fixes.
  • Political Ideology as Modernity’s Amplifier: US data shows liberals report 2–3x higher depression/poor mental health than conservatives (2022–2025 surveys: liberals average 53/100 mental health vs. conservatives’ 68). The gap widened post-2010 among youth, especially progressive girls. Not genetics alone (ideology ~50% heritable, but rates exploded while genes stayed fixed). Progressive focus on systemic chaos + social media contagion turns modernity’s meaning vacuum into a personal crisis. Conservatives’ emphasis on agency/tradition buffers it. Post-election swings prove it’s partly situational—but the baseline favors the right. This isn’t moralizing; it’s data showing how “infective thinking” hits harder on the left in a disconnected world.
  • Does Depression Pull People Left? (The Reverse Causality Question): Yes—longitudinal data show poor mental health and early distress predict shifts toward liberalism, not the other way around. Vulnerable kids (trauma, poor health, high neuroticism) become liberal adults; distress precedes “system-blaming” views, especially in girls post-2010. It’s a loop: modernity creates the vulnerability → people seek meaning in progressive empathy/identity → overload and vacuum worsen symptoms. Conservatism isn’t “coping” or denial—it’s protective factors (tradition, agency) that reduce the cry for meaning. Depression is real, but the epidemic isn’t politics—it’s the meaningless world pulling the vulnerable into amplifying ideologies. Turn toward purpose, not ideology or pills.
  • Does Losing an Election (or Politics Itself) Cause Depression? (The Post-Election Shift Question): Post-2024 data shows the losing side (Democrats) saw real drops in well-being, optimism, and control that lasted months—not just “sour grapes,” but identity fusion: politics now is self, turning every win/loss into existential crisis. Both sides get pissed because it’s invaded bedrooms, homes, and meaning. Solution? Depoliticize the personal—government handles roads and rules; keep private life private. Curate news (less overload), focus on local purpose and real connections. This isn’t denial; it’s reclaiming your life from modernity’s identity trap. Depression here is a cry for meaning beyond the circus.

“Studies show kids of LGBT parents turn out statistically better!"—pushed via activist reviews/small convenience samples of stable couples. Large-scale evidence contradicts. Mark Regnerus’ 2012 New Family Structures Study (nationally representative) found children of parents with same-sex relationships worse off on numerous outcomes vs. intact biological mother-father homes. A 2025 Cornell multiverse analysis tested over 2 million analytic variations on the data — every single model confirmed the “LGBT effect”: kids fare worse (or at best no better) with LGBT parents than intact bio parents. Biological two-parent homes remain optimal per decades of sociology; the “better” narrative is ideology overriding evidence.

Demonizing Institutions and Normalizing the Unnormalized

The modern left often portrays the state (or traditional institutions) as inherently oppressive or evil—e.g., “defund the police,” systemic racism baked into government, or viewing borders/national sovereignty as xenophobic. This demonization flips traditional good/evil: what was once normal (law enforcement, merit-based systems, national identity) becomes “oppressive,” while unchecked empathy or radical change becomes “justice.”

Examples include overusing “misogyny” and “transphobic” labels to shut down debate—e.g., calling concerns about women’s sports or biological definitions “hate speech” or “bigotry,” even when rooted in evidence or common sense. This creates a perverted moral framework where disagreement = evil, normalizing what was abnormal (e.g., rapid normalization of homosexuality as not just accepted but celebrated in ways that blend with fetishism or hypersexual culture).
